Smart Contracts were originally defined as computerized transaction protocols that execute terms of a contract. The contract exists within a blockchain network. They are designed to allow the participants of a transaction to be notified of the results immediately, without needing to involve any third party. A developer can design a smart contract by specifying a set of conditions that need to be met in order for a network of computers to execute the desired actions.
